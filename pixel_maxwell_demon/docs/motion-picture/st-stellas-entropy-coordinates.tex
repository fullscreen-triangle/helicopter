The temporal S-entropy coordinate system extends static spatial entropy to dynamic video sequences, providing a monotonic coordinate system orthogonal to chronological time.

\subsubsection{Four-Dimensional Temporal Entropy Space}

For a video sequence $\{I_t(x,y)\}_{t=0}^T$, we define temporal S-entropy coordinates at each frame $t$:

\begin{align}
S_k(t) &= -\sum_{i} p_i(t) \log p_i(t) \quad \text{(Knowledge entropy)} \label{eq:Sk}\\
S_t(t) &= \frac{1}{N}\sum_{x,y} \|\nabla_t I_t(x,y)\|^2 \quad \text{(Temporal gradient entropy)} \label{eq:St}\\
S_e(t) &= \text{H}[\mathbf{v}(t)] \quad \text{(Evolution entropy from optical flow)} \label{eq:Se}\\
S_{\text{cum}}(t) &= \int_0^t \dot{S}(\tau) d\tau \quad \text{(Cumulative entropy production)} \label{eq:Scum}
\end{align}

where $p_i(t)$ is the normalized pixel intensity histogram at frame $t$, $\nabla_t$ is the temporal gradient operator, $\mathbf{v}(t)$ represents the optical flow field, and $\text{H}[\cdot]$ denotes differential entropy.

\subsubsection{Entropy Production Rate}

The instantaneous entropy production rate captures irreversible information generation:

\begin{equation}
\dot{S}(t) = \frac{dS_k}{dt} + \alpha \frac{dS_t}{dt} + \beta \frac{dS_e}{dt}
\label{eq:entropy_rate}
\end{equation}

where $\alpha, \beta > 0$ are weighting parameters. The critical property is \textit{monotonicity}:

\begin{theorem}[Temporal Entropy Monotonicity]
For video sequences representing physical processes with energy dissipation, the cumulative entropy $S_{\text{cum}}(t)$ is strictly monotonically increasing:
\begin{equation}
\frac{dS_{\text{cum}}}{dt} = \dot{S}(t) > 0 \quad \forall t \in [0, T]
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Physical video capture records irreversible processes: photon absorption, sensor noise, molecular diffusion. Each contributes positive entropy:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Photon shot noise}: Poisson statistics → $\Delta S_k > 0$
\item \textbf{Thermal fluctuations}: Brownian motion → $\Delta S_e > 0$  
\item \textbf{Information loss}: Compression, quantization → $\Delta S_t > 0$
\end{enumerate}
Since $\dot{S}(t)$ is a weighted sum of positive terms, $\dot{S}(t) > 0$ almost everywhere, ensuring strict monotonicity of $S_{\text{cum}}(t)$.
\end{proof}

\subsubsection{Entropy-Time Bijection}

Traditional video establishes bijection $\phi: t \leftrightarrow n$ where $n$ is frame index. We instead construct:

\begin{equation}
\Phi: S_{\text{cum}} \to \{I_0, I_1, \ldots, I_T\}
\end{equation}

Since $S_{\text{cum}}(t)$ is monotonic, this mapping is well-defined but \textit{not invertible} in the traditional sense. Instead, we construct a \textit{dual-membrane inverse}:

\begin{equation}
\Phi^{-1}: S_{\text{cum}} \to \{I_{\text{front}}, I_{\text{back}}\}
\end{equation}

where $I_{\text{front}}$ and $I_{\text{back}}$ represent two conjugate frames at the same entropy level, analogous to amplitude-phase duality.

\subsubsection{Temporal Uncertainty Relation}

The dual-membrane structure induces a temporal uncertainty relation analogous to Heisenberg's principle:

\begin{equation}
\Delta t \cdot \Delta S_{\text{cum}} \geq \frac{1}{2}\hbar_{\text{info}}
\label{eq:temporal_uncertainty}
\end{equation}

where $\hbar_{\text{info}}$ is an information-theoretic constant and $\Delta t$ represents temporal precision. This relation forbids simultaneous exact knowledge of chronological time and entropy, forcing a choice between:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Front face}: Precise chronological time, uncertain entropy path
\item \textbf{Back face}: Precise entropy trajectory, uncertain chronological correspondence
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Entropy Coordinate Geometry}

The four-dimensional space $(S_k, S_t, S_e, S_{\text{cum}})$ forms a Riemannian manifold with metric:

\begin{equation}
ds^2 = g_{ij} dS^i dS^j = dS_k^2 + \alpha^2 dS_t^2 + \beta^2 dS_e^2 + dS_{\text{cum}}^2
\end{equation}

Geodesics in this space represent \textit{natural entropy-increasing paths}. When playback position decreases (backward scrubbing), the system finds the geodesic in entropy space that maintains $\frac{dS_{\text{cum}}}{d\lambda} > 0$ where $\lambda$ is path parameter.

\subsubsection{Computational Implementation}

For practical implementation, we discretize:

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{Compute Temporal S-Entropy Coordinates}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE \textbf{Input:} Video frames $\{I_0, I_1, \ldots, I_T\}$
\STATE \textbf{Output:} Entropy coordinates $\{(S_k^t, S_t^t, S_e^t, S_{\text{cum}}^t)\}_{t=0}^T$
\STATE Initialize $S_{\text{cum}}^0 = 0$
\FOR{$t = 0$ to $T$}
    \STATE Compute histogram $p_i^t$ from $I_t$
    \STATE $S_k^t = -\sum_i p_i^t \log p_i^t$
    \STATE Compute temporal gradient: $\nabla_t I_t = I_t - I_{t-1}$
    \STATE $S_t^t = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{x,y} \|\nabla_t I_t(x,y)\|^2$
    \STATE Compute optical flow: $\mathbf{v}_t = \text{OpticalFlow}(I_{t-1}, I_t)$
    \STATE $S_e^t = \text{H}[\mathbf{v}_t]$
    \STATE $\dot{S}^t = \frac{dS_k}{dt} + \alpha \frac{dS_t}{dt} + \beta \frac{dS_e}{dt}$
    \STATE $S_{\text{cum}}^{t} = S_{\text{cum}}^{t-1} + \dot{S}^t \cdot \Delta t$
\ENDFOR
\RETURN Entropy coordinates
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

The resulting coordinate system provides a monotonic parameterization of video sequences, enabling irreversible playback while maintaining all original visual information through the dual-membrane structure.
